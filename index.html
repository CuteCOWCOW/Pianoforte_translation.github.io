Samples for Forte Piano Restoration Model

Introduction

The first method is based on training multiple autoencoder pathways, one per musical domain. As shown in the figure, a universal encoder will convert the input waveform into a latent representation that eliminates the instrument domain information, and the wavenet decoder of each domain will restore the latent representation back to the audio of the corresponding musical instrument performance. And only During training, the domain discriminator is deployed to enforce encoder remove the instrument information.

The red columns are original through the encoder and the decoder of the same. This means that the red column should sound as close to the original as possible, while the other column should have a different timbre with the original one.
